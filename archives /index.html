5-Day Hypertrophy Program – Week 2 (Version 2)

Goal: Muscle building. Training days: Monday–Friday. Session length: 60–90 minutes. Structure: Warm-up (5–10 min) → Main workout (50–75 min) → Cool-down (5–10 min). Progression this week: add reps first, then small weight increases when sets feel comfortable.

MONDAY – UPPER PUSH A

Warm-up (about 8 minutes)

  • Light cardio (treadmill/rowing) – 3 min, easy pace
  • Arm circles (forward/backward) – 1 min total
  • Band pull-aparts – 2 × 15
  • Bodyweight push-ups – 1 × 10 (easy, just to activate)

1. Barbell Chest Press (Heavy)

Week 1 baseline: worked around 60–65 kg. In Week 2, keep 60–65 kg and aim to add reps toward the top of the range. Only increase to the next step (e.g. +2.5–5 kg) once you can hit the top of the rep range with RPE ≤ 8.

Weekly Progression Notes – Week 2

  • Keep the same exercises and general volume as Week 1.
  • Use your Week 1 weights as baselines and focus on adding a few reps across sets with good technique.
  • Only increase weight on a lift once you reach the top of the rep range on all sets at about RPE 7–8.
  • If a day feels very hard or you are very sore, keep the weights the same or slightly reduce and just get the work in.
  • Finish all 5 days this week if possible; the main win is consistency plus small, steady progression.
